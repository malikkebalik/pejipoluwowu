Introduction 

As of January 2024, Stipe Miocic’s net worth is roughly $4 Million. 

Stipe Miocic is a Croatian American professional mixed martial artist and Firefighter-Paramedic.

Miocic is currently signed to the UFC. He is the two-time and current UFC Heavyweight Champion. 

Early Life 

Stipe Miocic was born on the 19th of August, 1982, in Ohio.

Even though the family lived in difficult, there was no dearth of necessities when Stipe was growing up and both his parents worked to provide their kids with a better lifestyle.

When he was 3 years old, his parents divorced and his father went back to Croatia. 

Career 

Since childhood, Stipe Miocic has been involved with sports, wrestling, and playing baseball and football while at Eastlake North High School. 

While at university, he received interest from Major League Baseball teams and played Division II baseball at Coker College as a junior. 

Although he could have joined any of the richest MLB teams, Miocic had other skills he chose to explore and hone. 

He also wrestled in the 197 lbs weight class for the Cleveland State Vikings, and he competed in the 2003 NCAA Division I Wrestling Championships.  

Miocic’s Early Career

While he was still in school, Stipe Miocic was made a training partner of Dan Bobish at Strong Style MMA Training Centre in Ohio. 

After finishing school, he returned to the training center once more, and while he started with mixed martial arts, he soon changed his focus to boxing. 

Despite only training for several months, his coach, Marcus Marinelli, stated he could beat boxers with years more experience. 

With eight months of training under his belt, he had already become a Golden Gloves boxing champion, reaching the quarter-finals in the nationals. 

However, inspired by the richest MMA fighters in the world, Miocic eventually turned back to mixed martial arts, which kicked off his career. 

A Talented Fighter

Once he had officially started his MMA career, Stipe Miocic won his first six fights by knocking out his opponents. 

In 2011, he signed a multi-fight deal with the UFC and made his debut against Joey Beltran, winning with a unanimous decision. 

He faced Phil De Fries in February 2012, winning the fight in the first round via KO, and earned the Knockout of the Night award. 

A few months later, he faced newcomer Shane del Rosario and won with a TKO in the second round. 

He lost his first fight in September 2012 to Stefan Struve via TKO, though both fighters won Fight of the Night honors.

The following year, he was scheduled to face Soa Palelei but ended up squaring off with Roy Nelson after Palelei was injured. 

After winning that fight, he took on Gabriel Gonzaga, winning by a unanimous decision with Junior dos Santos as his next opponent. 

Beating The Odds

While an injury sidelined Junior, Miocic fought Fábio Maldonado instead, winning via TKO and earning Fight of the Night honors. 

In December, he finally got to take on dos Santos, but lost the fight by unanimous decision, his second significant loss. 

He was expected to face Ben Rothwell at UFC Fight Night 76 in 2015, but Rothwell ended up pulling out. 

Instead, Miocic faced Andrei Arlovski who he defeated in the first round for a Performance of the Night bonus. 

Shortly after, he was poised to fight Fabrício Werdum at the UFC Heavyweight Championship until Werdum pulled out with a back injury. 

Eventually, the pair squared off in 2016, and Miocic dished out Werdum’s first loss since 2011 to win the championship. 

A Reigning Champion

Miocic defeated his title for the first time in 2016 against Alistair Overeem, and while he got knocked down, he came back to win via KO. 

At UFC 211, Stipe Miocic defended his championship title again, this time from Junior dos Santos, who he’d previously lost to by unanimous decision.

However, this time, Miocic dominated dos Santos to maintain his titles, taking down his opponent midway through the first round. 

In 2018, he faced Francis Ngannou at UFC 220 in one of the most exciting fights in UFC history and he walked away victorious. 

For his fourth title defense, he faced Daniel Cormier, the UFC Light Heavyweight Champion in what was dubbed a “super fight.” 

Unfortunately, he lost via a knockout in the first round, though it was controversial since Cormier had jabbed Miocic in the eye. 

Rematches And Comebacks

In August 2019, Stipe Miocic and Cormier had a rematch for the Heavyweight Championship title, and this tie Miocic won. 

However, he ended up being out of the game until 2020 due to retinal damage from another accidental eye poke from Cormier. 

Once back, a trilogy fight between Miocic and Cormier was set for August, which Miocic won, though both fighters suffered eye pokes during the match. 

The following year, he faced Francis Ngannou again at UFC 260 but lost during the second round via knockout. 

After a two-year hiatus, he was scheduled to face Jon Jones in November 2023, but the fight was scrapped after Jones suffered a pectoral injury. 

Despite not being as well-known as Conor McGregor, Stipe Miocic’s MMA record is definitely impressive and it’s made him one of the best in the sport.
