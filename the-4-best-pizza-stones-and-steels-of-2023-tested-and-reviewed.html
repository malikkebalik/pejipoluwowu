The 4 Best Pizza Stones and Steels of 2023, Tested and Reviewed

Publish date: 2024-07-15

When you buy through our links, Insider may earn an affiliate commission. Learn more

Crisp, browned crust and melty, spotted cheese make for the perfect pizza, but the results are often hard to achieve in your home oven. That's where pizza stones come in: These rectangular or circular slabs of relatively thick stone or metal absorb heat to cook pizza much faster than a pan or sheet tray, mimicking the the results of commercial or wood-fired pizza ovens.

To find the best pizza stones and steels, we tested several models firsthand, using them to churn out pizzas and loaves of bread from a standard home oven. We also spoke with pizza expert Andrew Janjigian, baking instructor and former resident bread expert at Cook's Illustrated Magazine, to learn more about what makes a great pizza stone. Our top pick is the FibraMent Home Oven Baking Stone 20x15, which has a generously-sized baking surface and balanced heat transfer for cooking both pizza and bread. If you're only looking to make pizza with your stone, we recommend checking out the The Original Baking Steel; it made the best pizza of the bunch, but the heat transfer was too hot for baking bread.

Learning how to make pizza in your home oven with a pizza stone takes practice, below we also outline expert tips and tricks for getting started.
