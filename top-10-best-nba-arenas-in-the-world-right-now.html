Top 10 Best NBA Arenas in the World Right Now

Publish date: 2024-09-11

If you are a fan of the National Basketball Association’s games and championships, then you are in luck.

In this article, we present you with the world’s top 10 best NBA arenas of this year.

Well, the classic and legendary basketball stars like Michael Jordan and Lebron James also played in some of these top 10 best NBA arenas.

So, make sure to go through this article and feed your curiosity on what makes them so special ever in basketball history.

Best NBA Arenas in the World Right Now

The lists are compiled through the world’s renowned certified sites such as Franchise Sports, and the top 10 best NBA arenas in the world right now are listed below.

10. Toyota Center

Toyota Center managed to add its name as the top 10 best NBA arenas globally as of 2023.

Besides, the arena was established in October 2003 with Toyota as its chief sponsor.

In addition, the world’s renowned Harry County Houston Sports Authority (HSHA) owns the deluxe Toyota Center.

Primarily, the stadium is located in Houston, Texas, and is a house of the Houston Rockets.

Moreover, the association is prominent for the animated NBA matches between Houston Rockets, Miami Heat, and Washington Wizards, including numerous battles among the NBA teams.

Furthermore, the luxurious corporation has access to 18,104 seats for a basketball game, 19,300 for concerts, and 17,800 for ice hockey sport.

Interesting Facts: Several rocks and pop concerts are held by many bands and artists such as Metallica, Tool, Red Hot Chilli Peppers, Selena Gomez, Harry Styles, and many more

9. FTX Arena

FTX arena, previously known as American Airlines Arena, managed to add its name as the ninth-best NBA arena in the world right now.

It was established in 1999 in Miami, Florida.

Besides, the arena is a home for the celebrated basketball league, Miami Heat.

Likewise, the versatile and lavish stadium has 80 extravagant suites and 2,105 NBA seats.

Also, the stadium hosted NBA finals for numerous years, from 2006 to 2014.

Furthermore, the league also conducted huge international concerts of spectacular pop artists such as Selena Gomez, Madonna, Shakira, including MTV Music Video Awards in 2004 and 2005, respectively.

Interesting Facts: FTX Arena held WWE’s four major pay-per-view events, which include Royal Rumble(2006), Survival Series (2007 & 2010), and Hell in a Cell(2013).

8. Amway Center

Amway Center managed to add its name as the world’s eighth-best NBA arenas.

Designed with classic basketball cultures and Floridian origin style, Amway Center is the house of Orlando Magic and Orlando Solar Bears.

Initially, the City of Orlando founded the venue on October 1, 2010. Besides, the stadium is located in Downton Orlando.

Moreover, the stadium is the world’s most technologically advanced venue in NBA history.

Furthermore, the venue has intermediate luxury club seats, including 18,846 NBA basketball seats, 17,192 football seats, 17,353 ice hockey seats, 17,030 center stage concerts, and 16,486 end-stage concerts.

Especially, the display boards inside the venue are prolific in terms of the qualitative, wonderful live game experiences and dynamic motion graphics.

7. Pepsi Center

Denver’s sophisticated NBA auditorium, Pepsi center, managed to add its name as the world’s seventh-best NBA arena, as of 2023.

Initially, an American billionaire entrepreneur Stan Kroenke founded the company in 1999.

Besides, the venue is home to many professional NBA teams, including Denver Nuggets, Colorado Avalanche, Colorado Mammoth, and Colorado Crush.

Likewise, the interior designs of the arena include portraits of stunning hockey and basketball players in action.

Moreover, the stadium has 19,309 club seats, 18,007 hockey seats, 17,417 football seats, and 25,000 concerts seats.

6. United Center

United Center is one of the greatest NBA arenas situated on the Near West Side of Chicago, USA.

In 1998, the world’s renowned Hockey league Chicago Blackhawks founders, William Wirtz and Jerry Reinsdorf founded the futuristic arena.

Besides, the arena is home to the professional National Basketball and Hockey League, Chicago Bulls and  Chicago Blackhawks.

Similarly, United Airlines certified its name on the arena. Moreover, the accessible seats in the venue are 20,917 for basketball,19,717 for hockey, and 23,500 for concerts.

Furthermore, the arena organized NBA Finals three times and Stanley Cups Final.

Also, the stadium conducted the NBA All-Star Game in 2020. Beyond that, the venue also hosted Road to Wrestlemania House Show on March 3, 2018.

Interesting Facts: United Center treasures the enigmatic 1994 statue of Michael Jordan around the venue.

5. TD Garden

TD Garden, a versatile arena in Boston, Massachusetts, managed to add its name as the fifth-best NBA arenas 2023.

Besides, the arena is home to NBA and National Hockey League professional teams, Boston Celtics and Boston Bruins respectively.

Founded in 1997, the arena conducted numerous prime national sports events, including the 2016 United States Figure Skating Championship, NCAA Division I Men’s and Women’s Basketball Tournament.

Also, the arena is a concert venue for several comedians such as Tim Allen and Chris Rock, including various national musical tours.

Interesting Facts: Approximately 3.5 million people visit the arena every year. Hence, TD Garden is the No.1 idolized multi-diverse entertainment arena in New England.

4. Moda Center

Portland’s prime indoor sports arena, Moda Center managed to add its name as the fourth-best NBA arenas.

On October 12, 1995, Microsoft’s co-founders, Paul Allen and Jody Allen’s founded the auditorium.

Besides, the stadium is home to Portland Trail Blazers, an NBA’s professional team.

Similarly, the arena has 20,500 seats for basketball and 18,280 seats for hockey and lacrosse.

Moreover, the stadium hosted three NBA Western Conference Final series and U.S. Figure Skate Championships, including the 1998 memorable Portland  State commencement ceremony.

Furthermore, the arena is a house for numerous entertainment events, including hockey, circuses, ice skating, concerts, and global conferences.

3. Barclays Center

Brooklyn’s prime indoor sports and entertainment arena, Barclays Center, managed to add its name as the world’s third-best NBA arena, as of 2023.

Besides, the arena is home to the NBA, Women’s NBA leagues Brooklyn Nets, and the New York Liberty.

Established in 2012, the arena is certified under the Barclays Bank since 2007.

Moreover, the arena has 17,7seats for basketball, 15, 795 seats for ice hockey, 16,000 seats for boxing and wrestling, and 17,000 for concerts.

Some of the notable events hosted in the arena include the 2013 NBA draft, 2015 NBA All-Star Weekend festivities, and ACC Men’s Basketball Tournament, 2015 Stanley Cup.

Interesting Facts: An iconic Brooklyn-based painter José Parlá painted a showpiece art entitled the mural Diary of Brooklyn in the building of Barclays Center.

2. Staples Center

Legendary NBA star Kobe Bryant‘s home Staples Center managed to add its name as the world’s second-best NBA arena.

On October 17, 1999, two associations Anschutz Entertainment Group and LA’s Arturo Arena Company, founded the magnificent arena, Staples Center.

Also, the arena is home to the National Hockey League’s Los Angeles Kings and Los Angeles Clippers.

Similarly, the auditorium has 19,060 seats for the Clippers, 18,997 seats for the Lakers, 21,000 seats for concerts, and 18,230 seats for hockey.

Some of the notable entertainment events conducted in the arena are 19 Grammy Award events seven NBA Finals, 2 Stanley Cup Finals, and three years Women Tennis Association’s Tour Championships.

On February 24, 2020, the venue conducted a memorial for Laker’s star Kobe Bryant and his daughter, who suddenly passed away due to a helicopter crash.

Interesting Facts: One of the world’s most excellent arenas hosts over 250 events with approximately 4 million guests every year.

1. Madison Square Garden

New York’s renowned versatile entertainment arena, Madison Square Garden is at the top-notch of the world’s top 10 best NBA arenas.

The oldest but exceptional arena is located in Pennsylvania Plaza, in New York City.

Initially, New York’s illustrious Madison Square Garden Entertainment founded the arena on October 29, 1964.

Some of the notable events hosted in the venue include NBA Finals, Stanley Cup Finals, two times NBA All-Star Games, and five Grammy Awards.

Moreover, the all-time treasured events of the arena include concerts of Michael Jackson’s Bad World Tour, Led Zeppelin, Lady Gaga, and WWE’s Royal Rumble match two times.

Interesting Facts: The historical poignant boxing match between Muhammad Ali and Joe Frazier was held in Madison Square.
