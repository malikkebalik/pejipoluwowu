'Love Is Blind' stars Micah and Paul ended season 4 with a messy breakup and things only got mes

Publish date: 2024-07-05

  • Micah and Paul were an unlikely pair when they got engaged in the "Love Is Blind" season four pods. 
  • They made it to the altar, but Paul told Micah he didn't want to marry her. 
  • On "After the Altar" season four, fans learned that their relationship got even messier after the reunion.

Micah Lussier and Paul Peden were an unlikely pair on "Love Is Blind" season 4. She earned a reputation for being a mean girl in the pods, and he's an environmental scientist with a nerdy edge.

But they made it down the aisle nonetheless — only to have Paul dump Micah at the altar in the finale. In the aftermath of his devastating decision, Paul explains to the cameras that he struggled to envision Micah as a mother because he didn't think she was "nurturing."

That sure seems like a pretty definitive breakup. But on the season four reunion, which was filmed and aired on April 16, fans learn that the formerly engaged couple continued to date each other for a while after the "Love Is Blind" cameras initially stopped rolling, before splitting again. And the new "After the Altar" episodes that premiered Friday revealed that things between the exes got pretty messy after their aborted wedding — even after Paul got in a new relationship.

Here's everything we know about Micah and Paul's relationship today.

'After the Altar' reveals Micah and Paul kept in touch after their split

On "After the Altar," Micah reveals that she's still friends with Paul's mom, Liz, and the ladies meet up to spill tea when Micah moves back to Seattle. She admits to her almost-mother-in-law that she believes there are "lingering" feelings between her and Paul and says she still talks to him, but neither of them wants to admit their emotions.

Paul also admits to his mom in a separate scene that he still has feelings for Micah, though he is dating another woman at the time. He claims that Micah was actually the person who officially ended their relationship for good when the "Love Is Blind" cameras stopped rolling. He wasn't ready to give up on the relationship yet, but Micah had moved from Washington to Arizona at the time and thought the distance between them was too hard.

He tells his mom that Micah said she'd be open to dating him if they ever "lived in the same place." Paul moved on, but hadn't cut off their communication. Throughout "After the Altar" Micah tells her friends that she and Paul have leaned on each other at different times, but she began feeling strange about their relationship since he was dating someone else.

On the final "After the Altar" episode, Micah and Paul have a conversation where she tells him that it's "hard and confusing" to keep talking to him while he's in a relationship with someone else. She tells him that she still loves him, but she has to cut off communication with him to move on with her life.

It's not made clear exactly when the "After the Altar" episodes were filmed, but Decider deduced that it was probably around May 2023 based on the inclusion of fellow contestant Chelsea Griffin's birthday celebration. Micah and Paul had some slightly confusing Instagram interactions in April, which led some to believe they may have been reuniting yet again.

But on May 30, Paul went public on Instagram with his apparent new girlfriend, a woman named Geneva Dunham. It's not clear whether Geneva was the woman Paul mentioned he was dating when he was still in contact with Micah on "After the Altar."

Geneva and Paul also went on vacation together in July, which is the last Instagram photo that appears on Paul's account. It's also not clear if Paul and Geneva are still together as of now, though there's no reason to believe they've broken up. So odds are, Micah and Paul are most likely done for good.
